0. Introduction

Toki Pona (lit. "The Language of Good") is a minimalist constructed language first published in 2001 by Sonja Lang ("jan Sonja"). The language originally consisted of only 120 words (the nimi pu suli), but new words have slowly been added over time by the community.


The following guide covers all grammatical aspects of the language. I have tried to vary the words used to introduce the reader to as many of the 120 nimi pu suli as possible, however not all 120 are featured.


For a more comprehensive dictionary reference, see this site by jprogr.

1. Pronouns & Verbs

Consider the following sentence:

s  v
mi moku I/we eat

Notice the verb moku (eat, drink, consume). Verbs do not change form depending on the context, unlike in English (I eat, he eats). moku will always be moku.


Next, take a look at the subject mi. mi is the general 1st person pronoun (I, me, we).


These are all the pronouns in toki pona:

pronouns
person t.p. word eng. equiv.
1st mi I/me/we
2nd sina you/you all
3rd ona s/he/it/him/her/they/them


The word mute (many, much, several, a lot) can be used to explicitly show plurality:

s    a    v
sina mute alasa you all hunt

Similarly, wan (one, a) can be used to show singularity:

s   a   v
ona wan lukin s/he/it sees

To show gender, use meli (f) or mije (m).

s   a    v
ona meli alasa she/they (f) hunt(s)
a   a    v
ona mije alasa he/they (m) hunt(s)

Naturally, you can combine gender and number adjectives for extra specificity:

s   a    a   v
ona meli wan alasa she hunts, one/a woman hunts

You may be wondering why it's ona meli wan and not ona wan meli. This leads us perfectly into section 2.



2. Adjectives & Bracketing

By default, adjectives are bracketed in the following way:

  n   a     a     a
((jan meli) lili) pona friendly young woman

Sometimes you may want to change the way adjectives are bracketed. For example, let's try to express jan Sonja's Reddit flair ("mother of Toki Pona") with the default bracketing:

n     n     a
(mama toki) pona friendly mother of language

See the problem? What we meant to say was "mother of Toki Pona" but due to the default adjective bracketing, we qualified mama toki with pona instead of qualifying mama with the entire phrase toki pona.


Here's the correct solution:

n    pa  n    a
mama pi (toki pona)
