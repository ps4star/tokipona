1. Pronouns & Verbs

Consider the following sentence:

s  v
mi moku I/we eat

Notice the verb moku (eat, drink, consume). Verbs do not change form depending on the context, unlike in English (I eat, he eats). moku will always be moku.


Next, take a look at the subject mi. mi is the general 1st person pronoun (I, me, we).


These are all the pronouns in toki pona:

person t.p. word eng. equiv.
1st mi I/me/we
2nd sina you/you all
3rd ona s/he/it/him/her/they/them

The word mute (many, much, several, a lot) can be used to explicitly show plurality:

s    a    v
sina mute alasa you all hunt

Similarly, wan (one, a) can be used to show singularity:

s   a   v
ona wan lukin s/he/it sees

To show gender, use meli (f) or mije (m).

s   a    v
ona meli alasa she/they (f) hunt(s)
a   a    v
ona mije alasa he/they (m) hunt(s)

Naturally, you can combine gender and number adjectives for extra specificity:

s   a    a   v
ona meli wan alasa she hunts, one woman hunts

You may be wondering why it's ona meli wan and not ona wan meli. This leads us perfectly into section 2.
